Non Constitutional Bodies

Non Constitutional bodies are those that are not mentioned in the constitution. They are either created by an act of legislature (i.e., a statutory body) or by an executive action or resolution (i.e, an executive body).

The Non Constitutional Bodies include:

  • NITI Aayog
  • National Human Rights Commission
  • State Human Rights Commission
  • Central Information Commission
  • State Information Commission
  • Central Vigilance Commission
  • Central Bureau of Investigation
  • Lokpal and Lokayuktas
  • National Investigation Agency
  • National Disaster Management Authority

NITI Aayog

  • NITI Aayog was established on January 1, 2015 as a successor to the Planning commission (which formulated India’s Five-Year Plans).
  • NITI Aayog, like that of the Planning Commission, was also created by an executive resolution of the Government of India (i.e., Union Cabinet). Hence, it is also neither a constitutional body nor a statutory body.
  • NITI Aayog is the premier policy ‘Think Tank’ of the Government of India, providing both directional and policy inputs.
Composition
Chairperson Prime Minister
Governing Council Chief Ministers of all states and Lt. Governors of Union Territories.
Special Invitees Experts, Specialists and Practitioners with domain knowledge (nominated by PM),
Regional Councils convened by the PM and comprises of the CMs of States and Lt. Governors of Union Territories in the region to address specific issues impacting more than one state or a region. These are formed for a specified tenure.
Vice-Chairperson appointed by the PM.
Ex-Officio Members Maximum of 4 members of the Union Council of Ministers to be nominated by the Prime Minister.
Chief Executive Officer He is appointed by the Prime Minist for a fixed tenure, in the rank of Secretary to the Government India.

Note: The National Development Council (NDC) was the apex body for decision creating and deliberations on development matters in India presided over by the Prime Minister.

  • It was set up in 1952 to strengthen and mobilize the effort and resources of the nation in support of the Five Year Plans made by Planning Commission,
  • The Council comprises the Prime Minister, the Union Cabinet Ministers, Chief Ministers of all States or their substitutes, representatives of the Union Territories and the members of the NITI Aayog (erstwhile Planning Commission).
  • NDC (National Development Council) has been proposed to be abolished. But till date no resolution has been passed to abolish it.
  • Since the inception of NITI Aayog's Governing Council (which has almost the same composition and roles as NDC), the NDC has had no work assigned to it nor did it have any meetings.

National Human Rights Commission

National Human Rights Commission
Nature of body A statutory body (Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993)
Functions
The commission is the watchdog of human rights in the country, that is, the rights relating to life, liberty, equality and dignity of the individual guaranteed by the Constitution or embodied in the international covenants and enforceable by courts in India.
Compostition
  • A chairperson and
  • Five members

In addition to these full-time members, the commission also has seven ex-officio members: the chairpersons of:

  1. the National Commission for Minorities
  2. the National Commission for SCs
  3. the National Commission for STs
  4. the National Commission for Women
  5. the National Commission for BCs
  6. the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights
  7. Chief Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities.
Qualifications
  • The chairperson should be a retired chief justice of India or a judge of the Supreme Court .
  • The members should be a serving or retired judge of the Supreme Court, a serving or retired chief justice of a high court.
  • Three of those five persons (out of which atleast one should be a woman) should have knowledge or practical experience with respect to human rights.
Appointed by The President
Term
  • The chairperson and members hold office for a term of three years or until they attain the age of 70 years, whichever is earlier.
  • The president can remove the chairperson or any member from the office on the grounds mentioned in below note.
Further Appointment They are eligible for re-appointment for same post but not eligible for other employment under the Central or a state government.

Note: Grounds of Removal for National and State Human Rights Commission and Central Vigilance Commission

  • If he is adjudged an insolvent or
  • If he engages, during his term of office, in any paid employment outside the duties of his office or
  • If he is unfit to continue in office by reason of infirmity of mind or body or
  • If he is of unsound mind and stand so declared by a competent court or
  • If he is convicted and sentenced to imprisonment for an offence.

State Human Rights Commission

State Human Rights Commission
Nature of Body A Statutory body (Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993)
Function
Inquire into violation of human rights only in respect of subjects mentioned in the State List (List-II) and the Concurrent List (List-III) of the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution.
Composition
  • A Chairperson and
  • Two members
Qualifications
  1. The chairperson should be a retired Chief Justice or a Judge of a High Court.
  2. The members should be a serving or retired judge of a High Court or a District Judge in the state with a minimum of seven years experience as District Judge and a person having knowledge or practical experience with respect to human rights.
Appointed By Governor
Term
  1. The chairperson and members hold office for a term of three years or until they attain the age of 70 years, whichever is earlier.
  2. The president (and not by the governor) can remove the chairperson or any member from the office on the grounds mentioned in above note.
Further Appointment They are eligible for re-appointment for same post but not eligible for other employment under the Central or a state government.

Central Information Commission

Central Information Commission
Nature of Body A Statutory Body (Right to Information Act, 2005)
Functions
  • The Central Information Commission is a high-powered independent body which inter alia looks into the complaints made to it and decide the appeals.
  • It entertains complaints and appeals pertaining to offices, financial institutions, public sector undertakings, etc., under the Central Government and the Union Territories.
Composition
  • A Chief Information commissioner
  • Not more than ten Information Commissioners.
Qualifications
  • They should be persons of eminence in public life with wide knowledge and experience in law, science and technology, social service, management, journalism, mass media or administration and governance.
  • They should not be a Member of Parliament or Member of the Legislature of any State or Union Territory. They should not hold any other office of profit or connected with any political party or carrying on any business or pursuing any profession.
Appointed by President
Term
  • The Chief Information Commissioner and an Information Commissioner shall hold office for such term as prescribed by the Central Government or until they attain the age of 65 years, whichever is earlier.
  • The President can remove the Chief Information Commissioner or any Information Commissioner from the office on the grounds mentioned in below note.
Further Appointment They are not eligible for reappointment.

Note: Grounds of Removal for Central and State Information Commission

  1. if he is adjudged an insolvent or
  2. if he has been convicted of an offence which (in the opinion of the President) involves a moral turpitude or
  3. if he engages during his term of office in any paid employment outside the duties of his office or
  4. if he is (in the opinion of the President) unfit to continue in office due to infirmity of mind or body or
  5. if he has acquired such financial or other interest as is likely to affect prejudicially his official functions.

They can also be removed on the ground of proved misbehaviour or incapacity . However, in these cases, the President has to refer the matter to the Supreme Court for an enquiry. If the Supreme Court, after the enquiry, upholds the cause of removal and advises so, then the President can remove him.

State Information Commission

State Information Commission
Nature of Body A Statutory Body (Right to Information Act of 2005)
Functions
  • The State Information Commission is a high-powered independent body which inter alia looks into the complaints made to it and decide the appeals.
  • It entertains complaints and appeals pertaining to offices, financial institutions, public sector undertakings, etc., under the concerned state government.
Composition
  • State Chief Information Commissioner
  • Not more than ten State Information Commissioners
Qualifications
  • They should be persons of eminence in public life with wide knowledge and experience in law, science and technology, social service, management, journalism, mass media or administration and governance.
  • They should not be a Member of Parliament or Member of the Legislature of any State or Union Territory. They should not hold any other office of profit or connected with any political party or carrying on any business or pursuing any profession.
Appointed by The Governor
Term
  • The State Chief Information Commissioner and a State Information Commissioner shall hold office for such term as prescribed by the Central Government or until they attain the age of 65 years, whichever is earlier. They are not eligible for reappointment.
  • The Governor can remove the State Chief Information Commissioner or any State Information Commissioner from the office on the grounds mentioned in above note.
Further Appointment They are not eligible for reappointment.

Central Vigilance Commission

Central Vigilance Commission
Nature of Body Initially an Executive Body (1964), now an Statutory Body (2003)
Functions The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) is the main agency for preventing corruption in the Central government.
Composition
  • A Central Vigilance Commissioner (chairperson)
  • Not more than two vigilance commissioners.
Appointed by The President
Term
  • They hold office for a term of four years or until they attain the age of sixty five years, whichever is earlier.
  • The president can remove the Central Vigilance Commissioner or any vigilance commissioner from the office on the grounds mentioned in previous note.
Further Appointment After their tenure, they are not eligible for further employment under the Central or a state government.

Central Bureau of Investigation

Central Bureau of Investigation
Nature of Body An Executive Body
Functions
  • The CBI is the main investigating agency of the Central Government.
  • It plays an important role in the prevention of corruption and maintaining integrity in administration.
Composition
  • Director
  • Special director or an additional director
  • Joint directors, deputy inspector generals, superintendents of police and all other usual ranks of police personnel.

Note: The establishment of the CBI and Central Vigilance Commission was recommended by the Santhanam Committee on Prevention of Corruption (1962–1964).

National Investigation Agency

National Investigation Agency
Nature of Body A Statutory Body (National Investigation Agency Act, 2008 )
Functions It is the central counter-terrorism law enforcement agency in the country.
Composition headed by Director-General appointed by the central government.

Note: The NIA was established in the backdrop of the 2008 Mumbai terror attacks, popularly known as the 26/11 incident. This national horror led to the realization of the need for a separate federal agency to deal with terror-related crimes in the country.

Lokpal and Lokayuktas

Lokpal and Lokayuktas
Nature of Bodies Statutory bodies (Lokpal and Lokayukta Act, 2013)
Functions Lokpal for the Union and Lokayukta for States perform the function of an "ombudsman” and inquire into allegations of corruption against certain public functionaries and for related matters.
Appointed By Lokpal members by President and Lokayukta members by Governor

National Disaster Management Authority

National Disaster Management Authority
Nature of Body A Statutory Body (Disaster Management Act, 2005)
Functions The NDMA has the responsibility for laying down the policies, plans and guidelines for disaster management for ensuring timely and effective response to disaster
State Disaster Management Authority
Functions An SDMA has the responsibility for laying down policies and plans for disaster management in the state.
Composition
  • Every state government should establish a State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA) for the state. An SDMA consists of a chairperson and other members, not exceeding nine.
  • The Chief Minister of the state is the ex-officio chairperson of the SDMA.
  • The chairperson of the State Executive Committee is the ex-officio member of the SDMA.
  • The other members, not exceeding eight, are nominated by the chairperson of the SDMA.
  • The chairperson of the SDMA designates one of the members as the vice-chairperson of the SDMA.
  • The chairperson of the State Executive Committee acts as the ex-officio chief executive officer of the SDMA.
