Introduction to Union / Central Government

Part V

(Articles 52 - 151)

Part V at a glance

Part V Articles 52 - 151
Chapter I - The Executive 52 to 78
Chapter II - Parliament 79 to 122
Chapter III - Legislative Powers of President 123
Chapter IV - The Union Judiciary 124 to 147
Chapter V - Comptroller and Auditor-General of India 148 to 151

In this part, we are going to learn about the Union Government. A government has three main organs:

  • The Executive
  • The Legislative
  • The Judiciary

The Executive

Articles 52 to 78 in Part V of the Constitution deals with the Union executive.

The Union Executive consists of:

  • The President
  • The Vice-President
  • The Prime Minister
  • The Council of Ministers
  • The Attorney General of India

The Legislative

Articles 79 to 122 in Part V of the Constitution deals with Legislative i.e, the Parliament.

The Parliament of India is a bicameral legislature consisting of: .

  • the Rajya Sabha (also known Council of States or the Upper House).
  • the Lok Sabha (also known House of the People or the Lower House)

The Judiciary

Articles 124 to 147 in Part V of the Constitution deals with the Judiciary i.e., the Supreme Court.

Note

We will look at the Attorney General of India and the Comptroller and Auditor-General of India under Constitutional bodies.
