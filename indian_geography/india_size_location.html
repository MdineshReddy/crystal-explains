India - Size and Location

Location

India is a vast country. Lying entirely in the Northern and Eastern hemispheres. The main land extends between latitudes 8°4'N and 37°6'N and longitudes 68°7'E and 97°25'E.

The Tropic of Cancer (23° 30'N) divides the country into almost two equal parts. It passes through 8 Indian states: Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Tripura and Mizoram.

From the values of latitudes, it is understood that the southern part of the country lies within the tropics and the northern part lies in the sub-tropical or warm temperate zone. This location is responsible for large variations in landforms, climate, soil types and natural vegetation in the country.

To the southeast and southwest of the mainland, lie the Andaman and Nicobar islands and the Lakshadweep islands in Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea respectively.

From Gujarat to Arunachal Pradesh, there is a time lag of two hours. Hence, time along the Standard Meridian of India (82°30'E) passing through Mirzapur (in Uttar Pradesh) is taken as the Indian standard time (IST) for the whole country. It is 5:30 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).

Extreme Points of India
Northernmost Point (Disputed and Administered) Indira Col, located in Jammu and Kashmir. Indira Col is a mountain pass on the Indira Ridge in the Siachen Glacier in Karakoram Mountains.
Easternmost Point (Disputed and Administered) Kibithu, a town in the district of Anjaw in Arunachal Pradesh.
Southernmost Point Indira Point, located in the Nicobar District of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
Southernmost Point (Mainland) Cape Comorin in KanyaKumari District of Tamil Nadu.
Westernmost Point Guhar Moti in Kutch, near the Sir Creek (disputed), Gujarat
Highest Point (Disputed) K2 (8,611 m above sea level) in the Karakoram range.
Highest Point (Undisputed) Kangchenjunga ( 8,586 m above sea level) in Himalayas.
Highest Point (Undisputed and entirely within India) Nanda Devi (7,816 m above sea level)
Lowest Point Kuttanad (2.2 m below the sea level) in the Alappuzha district in the state of Kerala.
Coldest Place Dras in the Kargil district of the union territory of Ladakh (Average temperatures: −20 °C in Winter and 23 °C in Summer).
Hottest Place Jaisalmer, Rajasthan (average temperature in Summer is around 49 °C)
Driest Place Leh, Jammu and Kashmir (102 mm of average annual rainfall).
Wettest Place Mawsynram, Meghalaya (11,872 mm of average annual rainfall).

Size

India is the seventh-largest country in the world, with a total area of 3.28 million square kilometres. India’s total area accounts for about 2.4 per cent of the total geographical area of the world.

India measures 3,214 km from north to south and 2,933 km from east to west. India has a land boundary of about 15,200 km and the total length of the coastline of the mainland, including Andaman and Nicobar and Lakshadweep, is 7,516.6 km.

India's territorial waters extend into the sea to a distance of 12 nautical miles (22.2 km; 1 Nautical Mile = 1.852 Km) from the coast baseline.

Neighbours of India

India has 7 land bordering countries and two maritime bordering countries:

  • Pakistan and Afghanistan in the northwest, China (Tibet), Nepal and Bhutan in the north;
  • Myanmar and Bangladesh in the east;
  • Sri Lanka and Maldives, across the sea in south. Sri Lanka is separated from India by a narrow channel of sea formed by the Palk Strait and the Gulf of Mannar, while Maldives Islands are situated to the south-west of the Lakshadweep Islands.

The Himalayas together with other ranges, have acted as a formidable physical barrier in the past. Except for a few mountain passes such as the Khyber, the Bolan, the Shipkila, the Nathula, the Bomdila etc. it was difficult to cross it.

Demarcation Lines

  • McMahon Line: Proposed at the Shimla Convention of 1914, this line separates Tibet and the north-east India. It was accepted by Tibetan authorities and British India, and is now acknowledged by the Republic of India as the official boundary. But China rejected the Simla Accord and the McMahon Line, contending that Tibet was not a sovereign state and therefore did not have the power to conclude treaties. It forms the northern boundary of Arunachal Pradesh in the eastern Himalayas administered by India but claimed by China. The area was the focus of the 1962 Sino-Indian War.
  • Radcliffe Line: This line divided British India into India and Pakistan. It was drawn between West Pakistan (now Pakistan) and India on the western side and between India and East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) on the eastern side of the subcontinent.
  • Line of Actual Control (LAC): separates Indian-controlled territory from Chinese-controlled territory in the Sino-Indian border dispute. It is divided into three sectors: the eastern sector which spans Arunachal Pradesh and Sikkim, the middle sector in Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh, and the western sector in Ladakh.
  • Line of Control (LOC): is a military control line between the Indian and Pakistani controlled parts of the former princely state of Jammu and Kashmir. It is a ceasefire line and not an international boundary.

Political Geography

India is divided into 28 States and 8 union territories including the National capital territory (i.e., Delhi).
