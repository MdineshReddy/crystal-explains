Poverty & Unemployment

Poverty

Poverty is a state or condition in which a person or community lacks the financial resources and essentials for a minimum standard of living. Poverty means that the income level from employment is so low that basic human needs can't be met. Poverty-stricken people and families might go without proper housing, clean water, healthy food, and medical attention.

Types of Poverty

  • Absolute Poverty: Absolute poverty is a condition characterized by severe deprivation of basic human needs, including food, safe drinking water, sanitation facilities, health, shelter, education and information.
  • Relative Poverty: Relative poverty is a measure of income inequality. Usually, relative poverty is measured as the percentage of population with income less than some fixed proportion of median income. Also, there are other different income inequality metrics, e.g., the Gini Coefficient.
  • Situational Poverty: It is temporary in nature and occurs due to some adverse situation like bad crop, earthquakes, floods or some severe health problem.
  • Generational or Chronic Poverty: This type of poverty is inherited by household. The two to three generation born into poverty and they do not have tools to come out of this situation.

Estimation

The conventional approach to measuring poverty is to specify a minimum expenditure (or income) required to purchase a basket of goods and services necessary to satisfy basic human needs. This expenditure is called the poverty line. The basket of goods and services necessary to satisfy basic human needs is the Poverty Line Basket (PLB). Poverty can be measured in terms of the number of people living below this line.

The poverty ratio in India has been measured from a determined poverty line quantified in terms of per capita consumption expenditure over a month. Households with consumption expenditures below the poverty line are said to be “Below the Poverty Line (BPL)” and deemed poor.

The per capita consumption expenditure level which meets the average per capita daily requirement of 2,400 calories in rural areas and 2,100 calories in urban areas, along with a minimum of non-food expenditure, is called poverty line or absolute poverty.

World Bank Poverty Line: Presently, the World Bank defines extreme poverty as living on less than $1.90 a day, measured in 2011 purchasing power parity prices.

Unemployment

Unemployment is a situation when a person actively searches for a job and is unable to find work. Unemployment indicates the health of the economy.

Types of Unemployment

Structured Unemployment

Workers are out of work because there's no demand for that specific type of labor. It is due to mismatch between available and required skill set in the economy. Generally, it is due to technological innovation when the skill set of a person becomes obsolete. It also includes technological unemployment, where workers are replaced by machines.

Seasonal Unemployment

During the peak agricultural seasons (when the crop is ready for harvesting), more people are required for work. Similarly, in the sowing, weeding and transplantation period, more labor is required. Employment therefore increases at this time.

However, once these seasons are over, the agricultural workers, especially those who do not own land or whose land is not sufficient to meet their basic requirement (these are landless laborer and marginal farmers respectively), remain unemployed. This type of unemployment is known as Seasonal Unemployment.

Disguised Unemployment

If a person does not contribute anything in the production process or in other words, if he can be removed from the work without affecting the productivity adversely, he will be treated as disguisedly unemployed.

The marginal productivity of such unemployed person is zero. This occurs when more than the necessary number of people are employed for the specified work. Agriculture sector of underdeveloped/ developing economics possess this type of unemployment at a large scale.

Frictional unemployment:

Frictional unemployment is the time period between jobs when worker is transitioning from one job to another. It is sometimes called search unemployment and can be voluntary based on the circumstances of the unemployed individual.

Cyclical Unemployment

This type of unemployment is due to the recession in the economy. During recession, there is less requirement of man power on account of the decrease in the level of economic activities and thus, causes cyclical unemployment. This type of unemployment is prevalent in the developed countries.

An economy is considered to be at full employment when there's only frictional and structural unemployment. This is called the natural rate of unemployment.
